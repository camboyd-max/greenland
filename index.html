Karrat Fjord Landslide (2017)

A steep, south facing slope overlooks the Karrat Fjord. On June 17, 2017, 58 million cubic meters of rock was dumped into the water below. This created a tsunami with an initial wave height of 100 meters. By the time waves reached the nearby village Nuugaatsiaq, they were around 9 meters tall. This caused 4 casualties and extensive property damage. Illorlisat, another nearby village, was also impacted by the tsunami. Many residents had to resettle somewhere else. As of 2020, both villages are abandoned settlements because Greenland’s government declared them as dangerous zones.
